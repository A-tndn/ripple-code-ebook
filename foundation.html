Chapter 2: Core Concepts & Terminology

The dictionary of ripple mechanics - master these terms to see the system

üéØ Why This Dictionary Matters

Before we can work with ripples, we need a shared language. Each term here is not just a definition‚Äîit's a tool. If you misunderstand one, the whole framework collapses.

üß† Neuroscience Insight

Language shapes perception. Once you name something, you start to see it everywhere. This isn't mystical‚Äîit's how the brain's pattern recognition system works.

üîë Core Terms

1. Ripple

The smallest unit of influence. The shift created by any signal‚Äîthought, action, or event‚Äîas it moves outward into a system.

Examples: Drop a stone in water ‚Üí circles expand outward (ripple). Speak a word in anger ‚Üí tension spreads into a room (ripple).

2. Cause

The origin point of a ripple. Can be intentional (a decision, a trade) or unintentional (a careless word, a random delay).

Key insight: Causes don't need to be big. Often the smallest cause creates the largest echo, because it hits a system already unstable.

3. Carrier

The medium through which a ripple travels. In personal life: people, conversations, digital messages. In markets: media, rumors, algorithms.

Critical: Same cause through different carriers = different outcomes.

4. Echo

The return of a ripple‚Äîthe system's answer. May return directly, indirectly, delayed, or distorted.

Example: Criticize someone face-to-face (direct echo: argument). Criticize them in group chat (indirect echo: spreads wider, escalates).

5. Loop

A ripple that repeats through cycles. 3-day emotional loop in relationships. 7-day news cycle. Market echoes that recur monthly or yearly.

Loops are the building blocks of predictable patterns.

6. Frequency

Every ripple carries a tone‚Äîa vibrational signature. Joy, fear, urgency, neutrality. Two ripples on the same frequency will amplify each other; opposite frequencies cancel.

Physics parallel: Constructive vs. destructive interference.

üéØ Advanced Terms

Signature

The unique imprint of the ripple's source‚Äîlike a fingerprint. Even when masked, subtle elements reveal who initiated it.

Deviation

A break from the expected timeline. When the predicted ripple doesn't return as expected‚Äîsignals hidden forces at play.

üß™ Practical Drill: Ripple Dictionary in Action

Instructions:

  2. Log five entries throughout the day, noting:
    • Cause: What triggered it?
    • Carrier: Who/what carried it?
    • Echo: What came back?
    • Frequency: What was the tone?
    • Signature: Who initiated?
  3. Review at night: See if any loops or deviations appeared.

Do this for 3 days, and you'll find patterns that repeat across unrelated events. That's ripple literacy in action.
